The Grand Egyptian Museum

The crown jewel of modern curation. A monumental space dedicated entirely to the legacy of ancient Egypt, housing the complete Tutankhamun collection for the first time in history.

Prominent Collections

The Egyptian Museum

The historic pink palace in Tahrir Square, containing the world's most extensive collection of pharaonic antiquities spanning 5,000 years.

The Coptic Museum

Situated within the Roman fortress of Babylon in Old Cairo, this museum holds the largest collection of Coptic Christian artifacts in the world. It is famous for its beautiful stone carvings, intricate textiles (Coptic weaving), and ancient manuscripts.

Fustat Museum

he National Museum of Egyptian Civilization, now the permanent resting place of the Royal Mummies, offering a comprehensive view of Egyptian history.

Alex National Museum

Located in a restored Italian-style villa, this museum narrates Alexandria's history through the Pharaonic, Graeco-Roman, Coptic, and Islamic eras. It also features a unique section for underwater antiquities discovered in the Mediterranean Sea.

Royal Jewelry Museum

Housed in the glamorous palace of Princess Fatma Al-Zahra, this museum displays the private collections of the Muhammad Ali Dynasty. You can find diamond-encrusted crowns, gold tools, and precious medals that reflect the luxury of Egypt's former monarchy.

Museum of Islamic Art

One of the most important museums of its kind globally, it is located in the heart of historic Cairo. It contains rare masterpieces of woodwork, ceramics, and textiles from across the Islamic world, showcasing the artistic evolution of Islamic science and calligraphy.
