Tutankhamun’s Treasures:
The Complete Collection

For the first time since its discovery in 1922, the full funerary suite of King Tutankhamun is united under one roof. This exhibition is the crowning jewel of the Grand Egyptian Museum, offering an unprecedented look at the life, death, and afterlife of the "Boy King." It is not just a display of gold; it is a 100-year-old promise fulfilled, bringing together all 5,398 artifacts in a narrative-driven sequence.

📍 The Grand Egyptian Museum

Where the Golden Age of Kings meets the cutting-edge technology of tomorrow

Chronological Narrative: The tour follows the King’s journey from his childhood in the royal palace to his transition into a god in the afterlife.

State-of-the-Art Preservation: Artifacts are housed in German-engineered, ultra-clear glass cases that are seismic-proof and climate-controlled, allowing for an incredibly close look at details like the King's fingerprints on ancient pottery.

CURATOR'S ITINERARY

09:00 AM - The Hall of Chariots

Exploration of the six royal chariots, witnessing the military engineering of the 18th Dynasty.

11:00 AM - The Golden Shrines

A walkthrough of the four massive gilded shrines that once nested together to guard the King.

01:30 PM - The Inner Sanctum

Exclusive access to the centerpiece: The 11kg Solid Gold Mask and the innermost gold coffin.

03:30 PM - The King's Wardrobe

A close-up look at the King's personal jewelry, sandals, and the world’s oldest linen garments.
