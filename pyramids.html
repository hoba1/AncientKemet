Engineering Secrets of the Pyramids

Pyramid Architectural Description Current Status
Khufu (The Great) Consists of 2.3 million stone blocks, designed with incredible precision to face the four cardinal points. It has lost its smooth outer casing but still retains its complex internal passages and burial chamber.
Khafre (The Middle) Built on a higher elevation to appear taller than Khufu's, and is famously guarded by the Great Sphinx. It is the only pyramid that still preserves a portion of its original limestone casing at its peak.
Menkaure (The Smallest) Features a lower casing made of luxury red granite imported from Aswan, giving it a distinctive color. A large vertical gash remains on its northern face, resulting from an ancient failed attempt to dismantle it.
